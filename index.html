About Us

Understanding that skills in reading and writing are crucial for empowered and responsible citizens, the Literacy Fellows program serves to advance literacy as a way to break cycles of poverty in Hampton Roads and beyond. Fellows work to develop their own writing ability, while collaborating with outside organizations and communities to gain knowledge of the far reaching implications of literacy.

Correlation Heatmap

Correlation heatmaps are a graphical respresentation of the strength of the relationships between multiple variables and whether certain variables have a positive or negative correlation. As the shade of a scale gets darker in the scale, it means that there is little to no correlation between the variables and as the shade of a scale gets lighter, it means that there is a stronger correlation between the variables. The variable "age" on the y-axis and the variable "body mass index (BMI)" on the x-axis has a lighter shade so age and BMI are strongly correlated.

Clustered Bar Graph

The x-axis represents the BMI and the y-axis represents the total strokes. There are 4 clustered groups in the bar graph (Underweight, Healthy Weight, Overweight, and Obese). Each colored bar represents a certain age group in the age category. The bar graph displays that a person who is in their seventies and is obese had 29 strokes in total. This finding suggests that a person who is in their seventies and is obese is at the highest risk of getting a stroke.
